ROCK-PAPER-SCISSORS

1. The game is played with three possible hand signals that represent a rock, paper, and scissors.
2. The rock is a closed fist; paper is a flat hand with fingers and thumb extended and the palm facing downward; and scissors is a fist with the index and middle fingers fully extended toward the opposing player.
3. Rock wins against scissors; paper wins against rock; and scissors wins against paper.
4. If both players throw the same hand signal, it is considered a tie and play resumes until there is a clear winner.

The hand signals are given simultaneously by both players. The ritual used to get players in sync with each other so they can deliver their throws simultaneously is called the prime.
